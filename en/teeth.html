Toothache is divided into:

  • Pain while eating something cold or hot.
  • Pain while eating candy.
  • Pain while biting
  • Pain with no cause
  • If the pain you have is of the first or second type, this means that there is a cavity that needs to be treated with a cosmetic filling.
  • If the pain is of the third type... that means that there is a crack in the molar that needs to be treated, or there is inflammation in the gums and it can be treated by cleaning the tartar.
  • If the pain is of the last type...in this case, the nerve is inflamed and needs immediate emergency treatment before it forms an abscess.

When Should You Make Your Child’s First Dentist Appointment?

You should visit the pediatric dentist when the child's first tooth appears at the age of 6 months and 12 months. 
This visit is to ensure the health of the teeth and to teach the mother how to treat the child’s teeth to protect them from decay

After extractions, why you shouldn't delay getting dental implants?

Because teeth are not only important in chewing food and maintaining your appearance and pronunciation of words and letters, they also protect the jaw bones from the corrosion that occurs after teeth extraction. 
The jaw bone is very important in the dental implant process. 

When should you change your toothbrush?

Every two or three months, or when distortion or a change appears in the shape of the brush bristles, or when the person suffers from a bacterial or viral infection, such as cold. 

How do you choose the right toothbrush for you?

Different types of toothbrushes can be used.

  • First, make sure to choose a small to medium head size so that it reaches all parts of the mouth, especially the back molars.
  • Secondly, the brush bristles should be soft to medium soft so as not to harm the tooth enamel or gums, especially in the presence of infections.

What is a space maintainer for children?

In the event that primary teeth are extracted due to severe tooth decay, the permanent teeth move into the blank space causing crooked or crowded teeth in this case a space maintainer should be placed in order the adjacent molars do not tilt into the space, which causes a reduction in the space available for the permanent molar, and so that we do not need orthodontics in the future.
